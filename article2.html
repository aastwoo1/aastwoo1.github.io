Not Sayin’ Just Sayin’

October 27, 2016

About a week ago I wrote a piece arguing that Donald Trump’s campaign strategy is intentionally designed to alienate voters. Today a report has come out explicitly describing team Trump’s voter suppression plan. A senior official with the Trump campaign told Bloomberg Businessweek, “We have three major voter suppression operations underway.” In particular this includes plans to actively work to disengage white liberals, millennial women and people of color.
The Hillary Clinton camp seems to have caught on to this as well, in the past few days Clinton has warned her voters against complacency and told them to ignore the polls and make sure they vote.
Trump’s plan to alienate voters is obviously antithetical to democratic rule and suggests a level of dangerous political narcissism virtually unheard of in any major contender for the presidency in American history. As much as I love being right and a step ahead in my observations, I must reiterate: if every rational person who feels “meh” about Clinton doesn’t bother to vote then every misinformed individual who feels passionately about Trump will elect him. This is Trump’s desperate plan and it is dangerously close to working.
And even though nobody reads my blog I would like to note for the record that I saw it first.
I’m not sayin’, I’m just sayin’. #JobMe
